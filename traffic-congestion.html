Case Study 01

Traffic Congestion at Local Junction

A junction-level systems review focused on delay, conflict points, and inefficient movement coordination.

Study Type
Urban mobility analysis
Primary Issue
Peak-hour bottlenecks and unmanaged turning behavior
Outcome
Operational and design recommendations for smoother flow

Problem

The junction experiences repeated peak-hour congestion, with queues spilling into feeder roads and reducing mobility across the surrounding neighborhood.

Observations / Data

  • Turning vehicles regularly cut across through-lanes, creating friction and short stoppages.
  • Informal roadside stopping narrowed the usable carriageway during busy periods.
  • Signal timing and road-user behavior appeared poorly aligned with actual demand patterns.

Key Insights

  • The observed delay was not caused by volume alone, but by conflict between movements.
  • Minor unmanaged behaviors compounded into system-wide inefficiency.
  • A small operational redesign could produce outsized improvement without major capital spending.

Root Cause

Congestion stemmed from weak lane discipline, unmanaged curbside activity, and a junction layout that did not clearly separate movement priorities.

Recommendations

  • Introduce clearer lane guidance and channelization for turning movements.
  • Restrict stopping near the junction during peak traffic windows.
  • Review timing or manual traffic management interventions for peak periods.
